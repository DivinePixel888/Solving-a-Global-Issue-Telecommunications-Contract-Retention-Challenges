# Solving a Global Issue: Telecommunications Contract Retention Challenges

## 📊 Project Overview

This project investigates retention and infrastructure challenges within the telecommunications industry, with a specific focus on contract management processes. It was developed as part of a collaborative exercise aimed at identifying real-world operational problems and proposing data-informed insights.

The core issue explored is the lack of clarity in contract structures and accountability systems, which contributes to customer dissatisfaction and retention challenges.

---

## 🌍 Problem Statement

Retention challenges within telecommunications infrastructure processes are often driven by unclear contract purposes and a lack of accountability. In South Africa, this issue is particularly significant, where approximately 6,000 customers are engaged in long-term (24-month) contracts.

These gaps in communication and system transparency lead to customer confusion, service dissatisfaction, and operational inefficiencies. As a result, there is a growing need to improve contract management systems and strengthen communication between service providers and customers.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

* Identify key drivers of customer retention challenges in telecom contracts
* Understand the impact of unclear contract structures on customer experience
* Highlight infrastructure gaps affecting accountability and communication
* Propose improvements to enhance customer-provider relationships

---

## 🔍 Key Focus Areas

* Retention and churn risk factors

## 📌 Outcome

This project highlights how structural inefficiencies in contract management systems can negatively impact both customers and service providers. It emphasizes the importance of transparent communication and improved infrastructure design in reducing churn and improving customer satisfaction.
